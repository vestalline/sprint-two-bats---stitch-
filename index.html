The Egyptian Fruit Bat: A Keeper of the Forest

menu_book About the Species

The Egyptian Fruit Bat (Rousettus aegyptiacus) is a key architect of Mediterranean and African ecosystems. Unlike most bats, they use a primitive form of echolocation alongside keen eyesight to navigate the twilight hours. Their specialized fruit-based diet makes them essential seed dispersers, traveling long distances to feast on figs, dates, and carob, ensuring the continued growth of diverse forests.

info Quick Facts

  • Wingspan 60 cm (24 in)
  • Weight 100 - 170g
  • Lifespan Up to 22 years
  • Status Least Concern

Global Distribution

Inhabiting diverse terrains from the Nile River Valley to the caves of the Levant, these bats are highly adaptable dwellers of the arid landscapes.
